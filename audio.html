Audio element

MP3 works on 99% but if use more than one (AAC, OGG), use the source element. Note that AAC must be in an MP4 container. You really only need Ogg Vorbis if you have Linux users. Either one of those may compress smaller than MP3 though. 


audio controls="controls"
source src="audio/ThemeSong.ogg"
source src="audio/ThemeSong.mp3"
A link to download the MP3 since you live in 1999 or use Lynx.
/audio

Video element

Use H.264 (MP4) for IE/Safari and VP9 (WEBM) for FF/Chrome 29+ (because it's half the bandwidth of VP8.) Put the "src" in a source element (which is self-closing.) 


video controls preload="metadata"
source src="/video/zombies.webm" type="video/webm" 
source src="/video/zombies.mp4" type="video/mp4"
Your browser doesn't support the video element... 
/video

The poster element lets you make a thumbnail, basically, for when you don't want to instantly load a giant file- preload="none" poster="/img/zombies.jpg"
