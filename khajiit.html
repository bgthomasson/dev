Khajiit subspecies

In general it seems there are three major types: Elven, biped feline, and quadruped feline. The latter two are themselves divided into three groups by size. It seems often they are further subdivided into 16 to 20 types, but for the purposes of this I sought simplicity. For further details on the various kinds of Khajiit, as well as culture etc, see this page.

For obvious reasons all quadrupeds have zero skills in those that require opposable thumbs- Archery, Handcraft, One Handed, Two Handed, Smith. They can learn some alchemy from eating ingredients. To make up for the lack of weapons, quads have higher Unarmed skill, as well as a bite attack. It is also worth noting that they do wear clothing, although obviously it requires some help to put on. They are sensitive to their bestial appearance and take pains to appear sentient, even with other Khajiit.

Ohmes

resemble Bosmer (aside from the tail!) and so can disguise themselves more easily, unless they have unique tattoos. Being less bestial, they have more charisma to other races, but are shorter than average (about 5 feet) and thus less strong. An Ohmes character's back story is inclined to include diplomacy and trade amongst men and mer.

Dagi

are a rare, small (3 to 4 feet) bipedal feline, native to the jungle where they live in treetops and treehouses. Dagi have an affinity for magic, and ride large quad Khajiit into battle slinging spells and casting buffs, so a Dagi character is suited best to stealth magic and unconventional tactics. Due to the heat and humidity of their home, natives of Tengmar Forest are habitually scantily dressed, which scandalizes conservative desert dwellers.

Suthay

are the common cat-men seen in Morrowind, and thus have base stats. They are inclined to be rogues, rangers, and other light to medium styles. Their fur ranges in color and pattern from tiger to leopard to cheetah. Most likely to have ancestors from MOrrowind as Dunmer preferred to enslave this type.

Cathay

are similar to Suthay, only larger(6-7 feet) and thus more inclined to mixing heavier tactics in with guerrilla styles.

Alfiq

appear to be house cats. Apparently they cannot speak normally, but can understand spoken language. They are reputed to have magical abilities and be used as spies and saboteurs.

Pahmar

are jaguar to tiger sized, swift and vicious. They are best suited to stealth kills, especially in forests where they can drag the victims into the trees.

Senche

Roughly the same height as the average Altmer, with an average weight comparable to that of twenty Altmer. Their forelimbs are thick and half again as long as their rears, giving them an apelike appearance. They are most effective as intelligent tanks against lightly armed and armored opponents who are easy to maul and trample while the Senche riders shoot and spear.

Roles and skills

Mage

  • Alteration manipulates the laws of nature and physical objects.
  • Conjuration summons creatures and equipment from other planes.
  • Destruction damages attributes.
  • Enchantment binds the energy of living beings to objects.
  • Illusion alters perception.
  • Restoration restores attributes.

Warrior

  • Archery: bows, crossbows, darts, blowguns
  • Block: use of shields
  • Heavy Armor: obvious
  • One Handed: daggers, axes, and other smaller weapons
  • Two Handed: broadswords, warhammers, spears, etc.
  • Unarmed: the use of fists and feet, claws and teeth

Rogue

  • Acrobatics: jumping, running, climbing, escaping
  • Disguise: the ability to maintain a false identity
  • Light Armor: obvious
  • Fraud: blackmail, forgery, embezzlement, insider trading
  • Security: lockpicking, traps
  • Sneak: stealth, sleight of hand, pickpocket

Peasant

  • Alchemy
  • Speech: barter, persuade, how well you learn new languages
  • Survival: ability to live off the land
  • Handcraft: working in mundane materials like wood, clay, cloth
  • Perform: singing, dancing, erotic
  • Smith: working with metals
